# Naming

## Why "Nucleus"

The name does four things:

1. **It evokes the brain.** *Nucleus accumbens* is the brain region
   most directly tied to the "hook signal" Nucleus optimizes for —
   anticipatory reward, the neural correlate of "this is worth my
   attention." It's the single most validated target in fifteen years
   of neuroforecasting research. Naming the product after the region
   the engine is built around is honest signaling.
2. **It evokes the engine itself.** The double meaning of "nucleus"
   as the **central core of a system** is the second pillar. Nucleus
   is the engine inside a host product — the central core that turns
   raw brand input into scored, edited variants.
3. **It is short, ownable, and memorable.** Five letters, three
   syllables, one stressed beat. Easy to type, easy to say in a
   meeting, easy to remember.
4. **It does not over-promise.** "Nucleus" makes no claim about
   marketing, virality, AI, or video. It's a quiet name. The product
   does the talking.

## What was considered

The name search ran through ~30 candidates across four naming
strategies. The shortlist:

| Name | Strategy | Why considered | Why not |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Striata** | Latin / anatomical | Evokes the striatum (NAcc is part of the ventral striatum); elegant; ownable | Too clinical; harder to pronounce; trademark conflict with a financial-services firm |
| **Thumbstop** | Literal / category-defining | The product's job is to stop the scrolling thumb. Memorable. | Too cute; ages poorly; doesn't survive a B2B sales conversation |
| **Hook** | Single-word category | Hook score is the #1 metric. Punchy. | Too generic; massive trademark conflict surface |
| **Loopcast** | Compound | Recursive loop + broadcast | Generic; "cast" suffix is overfished |
| **Cerebra** | Brain-anatomy | Premium feel | Trademark conflict with a tech company |
| **Affect** | AIM framework reference | Direct reference to the affect-integration-motivation model | Too academic; weak as a product name |
| **Salience** | Neuro term | Memorable | Too long; harder to brand |
| **Cortex** | Brain-anatomy | Memorable | Massive trademark conflict |
| **AIM** | Acronym for the academic framework Nucleus is built on | Direct reference to Knutson 2014 | Generic three-letter acronym; trademark mess |
| **Nucleus** | Brain-anatomy + engine double meaning | All four criteria above | Some trademark conflicts in unrelated domains |
| **NuclearBrand**, **NeuroPeer Studio**, **Brainstem**, **Ganglion**, **Synapse**, **Lobus** | Various | Considered briefly | Each rejected for one of the above reasons |

## The shortlist process

The user (the founder building Nucleus) reviewed four options and
selected Nucleus directly. The decision is recorded in the
[brainstorming spec](../superpowers/specs/2026-04-09-nucleus-vision-design.md).

## Trademark posture

"Nucleus" has trademark conflicts in several unrelated domains —
financial services, education, healthcare. None of the existing
"Nucleus" trademarks register in the AI software / video software /
marketing technology classes (USPTO classes 9, 35, 42).

The recommended posture:

1. **File a US trademark in classes 9, 35, and 42** for "Nucleus" in
   connection with AI-powered video generation, neuromarketing
   analytics, and software-as-a-service. Estimated cost $1,500–$3,000
   in filing fees + attorney time.
2. **Register the domains.** `nucleus.ai`, `nucleus.dev`,
   `getnucleus.com`, `nucleus.video` are the priority. `nucleus.com`
   is taken (by an unrelated software company); not pursuing.
3. **Operate under the name immediately** even before the registration
   completes. Common-law trademark rights begin with first use in
   commerce.
4. **If a conflict letter arrives**, fall back to a qualified mark
   such as "Nucleus AI" or "Nucleus by [host]" depending on the
   conflict's class.

The trademark filing is on the post-launch checklist, not a launch
blocker.

## Naming conventions

Once the name is fixed, a small style guide keeps internal and external
references consistent.

| Surface | Form |
|---|---|
| Product name | **Nucleus** (capital N, lowercase rest) |
| Possessive | Nucleus's (with apostrophe-s, not just apostrophe) |
| Logo lockup | Lowercase wordmark `nucleus` paired with the mark |
| Domain | `nucleus.ai` (preferred), `nucleus.dev` (technical surface) |
| Twitter / social handle | `@nucleus_ai` |
| GitHub org | `nucleus-ai` |
| Email | `team@nucleus.ai`, `security@nucleus.ai`, `legal@nucleus.ai` |

## Sub-product naming

If Nucleus eventually grows sub-products, the convention is
`Nucleus [Capability]`:

- Nucleus Engine (the core scoring + generation loop)
- Nucleus Studio (the host-product-embedded UI)
- Nucleus Reports (the standalone neural reporting product)
- Nucleus Bench (the open-source benchmark suite — see [research roadmap](../research/research-roadmap.md))

The "Nucleus + capability" pattern is common enough (Stripe Atlas,
Stripe Sigma, Stripe Climate) that it doesn't feel forced.

## What the name is not

- **Not "NeuroPeer."** NeuroPeer is the existing scoring service in
  the author's stack. Nucleus is the product that consumes NeuroPeer
  (and other analyzers). The two names coexist without overlap.
- **Not "ugc-peer."** The repo was originally called `ugc-peer` as a
  working name. The product name is Nucleus; the repo URL stays
  `nucleus` going forward.
- **Not co-branded with the host.** Inside a host product, the brand
  surface is "Multiply" (a verb-named feature inside the host) rather
  than "Nucleus by [Host]." The Nucleus brand is the engine; the
  in-product surface uses host-aligned naming.
