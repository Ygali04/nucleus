# Brand

Nucleus needs a brand identity that does three things:

1. **Stand on its own.** Nucleus is a real product, not a TruPeer
   feature with extra steps. The brand has to feel substantial enough
   to be referenced, cited, and bought.
2. **Live inside another product gracefully.** Nucleus shows up
   embedded inside a host product surface. Its visual identity must
   coexist with the host's brand without screaming for attention or
   feeling like a foreign object.
3. **Convey the technical claim.** Nucleus is a brain-grounded
   engine. The visual language should hint at neuroscience, precision,
   and the recursive feedback loop without being literal about it.

This section covers the name, the visual identity, the voice and tone,
and the in-product brand placement pattern.

## The brand in one paragraph

Nucleus is a calm, technical, high-precision brand. It speaks the
language of neuroscience and engineering without being academic. It
feels closer to Linear, Stripe, and Anthropic than to a marketing
SaaS. Its visual system is minimal: indigo accent on a white field,
rounded corners, soft shadows, no glass morphism, no playful
illustrations, no stock photography. Its voice is direct, evidenced,
and confident. It does not try to be cool. It is good at one thing
and the brand makes that obvious.
